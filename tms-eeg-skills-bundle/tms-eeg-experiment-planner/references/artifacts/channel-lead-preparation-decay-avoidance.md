## Purpose

Prevent pulse, recharge, discharge, decay, and lead-configuration artifacts before data collection.

## Preparation Rules

- Check active electrodes, gel amount, impedance/contact quality, and unstable channels.
- Orient electrode wires about 90 degrees relative to coil orientation when possible.
- Avoid wire loops near the coil and document cable routing.
- Inspect single trials online for pulse, recharge, discharge, decay, and early lead-related artifacts.
- For i-TEPs, require stricter lead/cable documentation and very high temporal artifact scrutiny.
- Separate the short TMS-pulse artifact from longer decay/back-to-baseline recovery when documenting early artifacts.
- Remember that decay can persist even with low impedance and careful setup; intensity and electrode/lead factors can still matter.

## Online Checks

- pulse artifact duration and recovery
- channel-wise early residuals
- whether decay artifacts track specific electrodes or wires
- whether small coil/wire changes reduce artifacts
- whether early topography could be lead-driven
- whether decay duration changes with stimulation intensity

## Output Rule

For session checklists, include explicit electrode/lead/cable preparation and online artifact checks before main data collection.
