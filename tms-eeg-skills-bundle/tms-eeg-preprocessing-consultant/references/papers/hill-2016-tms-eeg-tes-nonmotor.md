## Why This Matters For The Agent

Useful for explaining why TMS-EEG is attractive for probing non-motor regions where MEP-like readouts are unavailable.

## Main TEP Nature Points

Non-motor TEPs can offer a window into cortical responses, but interpretation is constrained by target, sensory confounds, artifacts, and lack of a simple peripheral benchmark.

## Methodological Tips And Nuances

For non-motor targets, require stronger control conditions and artifact scrutiny than motor cortex MEP-oriented designs.

## Agent Rules Extracted

- Load for non-motor TMS-EEG rationale.
- Warn that non-motor excitability claims need careful operationalization.

## Claims To Avoid

Do not treat non-motor TEP amplitude as equivalent to motor threshold or MEP amplitude.
