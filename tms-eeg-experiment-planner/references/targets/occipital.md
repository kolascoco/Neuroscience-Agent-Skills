## Planning Focus

Occipital TMS can produce strong TEPs and is relevant for natural-frequency questions, but visual state and alpha rhythm must be considered.

## Risks

- phosphene/visual sensation confounds
- alpha/state dependence
- overinterpreting oscillatory peaks

## Practical Advice

Track visual state, eyes open/closed, alpha peak, and possible SSVEP/state manipulation if testing natural frequencies.

Rosanova et al. 2009; local good-practice note.
