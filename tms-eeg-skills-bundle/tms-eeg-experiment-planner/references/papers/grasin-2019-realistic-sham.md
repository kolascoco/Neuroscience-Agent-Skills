## Why This Matters For The Agent

Conference abstract (Brain Stimulation 2019, abstract #210) proposing criteria and a concrete realistic-sham implementation. Use for sham *design* and blinding intent; ground any methodological detail in peer-reviewed sham cards (Gordon 2018) since this is abstract-level evidence only.

## Key Methods

Sets three criteria for an ideal placebo rTMS: (i) active and placebo coil positions over the scalp are the same; (ii) subjective somatic scalp sensation and acoustic artifacts are matched between active and sham; (iii) no physiological effect on the targeted cortical region. Implementation: synchronous sound masking via a monitor speaker plus synchronous 10-Hz electrical stimulation through electrodes fixed under the front edge of the coil to mimic scalp sensation; electrical amplitude titrated per non-naive subject. Tested in 9 subjects (mean age 34.2, SD 6.7), Neuro-MS/D stimulator, HF rTMS over left DLPFC (MDD protocol). Reported indistinguishability from real TMS in ~100% of TMS-naive and ~88.9% of non-naive volunteers.

## Planning Rules Extracted

Realistic sham must be planned as part of the protocol rather than improvised during analysis. Match coil position, acoustic, and somatosensory sensation; titrate the sensory surrogate per participant; and verify blinding (ask subjects to distinguish real vs. sham). Naive vs. non-naive status affects blinding success.

## Claims To Avoid

Do not cite this as a peer-reviewed methods source or quote effect sizes; it is a brief abstract. Realistic perceptual matching does not guarantee matched neural confounds (see Gordon 2018).
