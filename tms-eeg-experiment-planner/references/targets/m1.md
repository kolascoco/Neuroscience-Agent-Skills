## Planning Focus

M1 has RMT/MEP anchors, but TMS-EEG planning still needs online TEP/i-TEP QC, coil orientation checks, and artifact controls.

## Risks

- overreliance on RMT as dose
- TMS-evoked muscle/scalp artifacts
- i-TEP contamination by pulse/lead/muscle artifacts
- coil orientation dependence

## Practical Advice

Use RMT as a starting point, then inspect early local TEP/i-TEP and artifacts. Consider current-direction controls for i-TEPs. Use neuronavigation if session-to-session reproducibility matters.
